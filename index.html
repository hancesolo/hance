Why Electric Vehicles May Not Be as Green as They Seem

The Hidden Environmental Costs of Battery Production

Electric vehicles are often touted as the solution to our carbon emissions crisis, but the production of their batteries tells a different story. The process of mining lithium, cobalt, and other rare earth elements needed for EV batteries is energy-intensive and often environmentally destructive. These mining practices can lead to significant land degradation, water pollution, and even human rights violations in regions where these minerals are extracted. Furthermore, the carbon footprint associated with battery production can be substantial, sometimes offsetting the benefits of zero emissions during the vehicle's use
